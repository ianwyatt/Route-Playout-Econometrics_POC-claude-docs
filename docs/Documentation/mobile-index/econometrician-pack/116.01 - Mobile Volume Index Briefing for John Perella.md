## Summary

When a frame near the Stadium of Light shows 35x its normal footfall on the night of a Springsteen concert, Route's average-based audience doesn't move. The mobile volume index captures that variation — using O2 mobile device data to add hourly footfall adjustments to Route's base audiences. Across 833 campaigns, the indexed audiences are **5% higher** than Route's estimate. **73% of campaigns gain.**

We cross-referenced spike dates against real-world events: **85% matched confirmed events** — Premier League fixtures, Champions League nights, concerts, festivals. The four campaigns here cover distinct profiles: steady urban (+6.7%), event-driven (+26.3%), transport hubs (+19.0%), and large national (+10.1%).

---

## What the Mobile Index Is

Each frame gets an hourly multiplier derived from O2 mobile device footfall at Output Area level. The baseline is per OA x day-of-week x hour — so a Saturday 15:00 index of 1.5 means 50% more footfall than the average Saturday at 15:00 at that location. The multiplier is applied to Route's standard impacts.

**O2 data:** Apr–Dec 2024 (272 days). The playout-relevant window is 7 Aug – 14 Oct 2024 (69 days), mapped to 6 Aug – 13 Oct 2025 playouts with day-of-week preserved. The 2024 patterns are real, but specific date-events won't align year to year.

The four campaigns span 14 Sep – 12 Oct. Cross-referenced against confirmed fixtures, 85% of top spike dates matched.

All four campaigns overlap during 22–30 Sep (PL GW5–6), the busiest spike period. Saturday matchdays produce the broadest uplift — 6–8 clubs at home simultaneously. Champions League MD2 (1 Oct) is the only midweek spike. Domino's (18273) ends 30 Sep so misses GW7.

## Known Limitations

1. **OA-level granularity.** The index is per Output Area, not per frame. Frames in the same OA share identical index values. Old Trafford and Trafford Centre share an OA — match-day football and Saturday retail are indistinguishable.

2. **Stadium data gap.** Major stadiums (Wembley, Emirates, Stamford Bridge, Etihad) have OOH frames nearby but none carry mobile index data in their immediate Output Areas. Where adjacent OA data exists, it captures spillover: Emirates 70% match-day uplift, Stamford Bridge 38%.

3. **Year lag.** The mobile data is from 2024 applied to 2025 playouts. Day-of-week is preserved but specific events won't fall on the same dates year to year.

4. **Deviation from typical behaviour.** An index of 1.0 on a match day means match days are normal at that location — Route's base audiences should already capture that footfall. The index adds value where events are unusual for a location.
