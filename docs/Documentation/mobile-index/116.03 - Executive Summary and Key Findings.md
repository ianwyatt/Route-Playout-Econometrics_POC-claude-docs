# 116.03 - Executive Summary and Key Findings

**Date:** 11 March 2026
**O2 mobile data:** Apr–Dec 2024, 99.87M frame-hours, 16,265 frames
**Playout data:** 833 campaigns, ~69 days (Aug–Oct 2025)

---

## Executive Summary

Mobile volume indexing adjusts OOH audience impacts by real mobile device footfall at each frame. Across 833 campaigns it produces a **net +4.93% uplift** in total impacts. **73% of campaigns benefit**, with 44% gaining 5%+. Only 7.2% see decreases larger than 5%.

The unweighted average index across all 100M frame-hours is exactly 1.0 — the index doesn't inflate or deflate in aggregate. The **audience-weighted average is 1.049** because high-footfall periods coincide with high raw audiences, amplifying the effect as a multiplier. Off-peak hours have low index AND low audience, so they contribute little.

Frame placement drives the variance. Channel Four's 204-frame JCDecaux campaign gained +28.5% from indexing. Red Bull's single Ocean Outdoor frame lost 35%. The same budget delivers materially different real-world audiences depending on frame proximity to high-footfall locations.

---

## How the Index Is Built

The index is derived from **O2 mobile device people counts** at Output Area (OA) level, hourly:

1. For each OA × day-of-week × hour, a **baseline** is calculated as the average people count across all dates in the dataset (e.g., all Saturdays at 15:00 at that location)
2. **Index = actual count ÷ baseline** — a value of 1.5 means 50% more footfall than typical for that place, day, and hour
3. The index is applied as a **multiplier to Route's standard impacts** at each frame

### Key Limitation: Day-of-Week Normalisation

Because the baseline includes event days, **venues with regular events show modest index deviation**. A Saturday football match at Old Trafford is compared against the average Saturday at Old Trafford — which already includes ~10 other Saturday home matches. So individual matches appear as smaller deviations than they would against a non-event baseline.

This means the index responds most strongly where events are **unusual for a location**. A one-off Springsteen concert at Sunderland's Stadium of Light produced a 35x spike — because that venue rarely hosts 49,000-capacity events. At Wembley, which hosts events most weekends, even major fixtures produce modest deviation because events are already baked into the Saturday baseline.

This is by design: Route's audiences model average behaviour including regular events. The mobile index measures deviation from what Route already captures.

The unweighted average of all index values is exactly 1.0 — roughly half of frame-hours are above average, half below. The **weighted** average is 1.049 — explaining the ~5% uplift.

### Playout Window Verification (Aug–Oct 2024)

- **85% of top 20 spike dates matched confirmed events** — PL GW1–7, Champions League, Notting Hill Carnival, Reading Festival, Billy Joel at Cardiff

### Stadium Data Gap

All four major stadiums assessed (Wembley, Emirates, Stamford Bridge, Etihad) have OOH frames within 200m but none carry mobile index data in their immediate Output Areas. Where adjacent OA data exists, genuine fan spillover is captured (Emirates 70%, Stamford Bridge 38%).

### Non-Venue Spikes

58% of extreme spikes (>5x) occur far from any stadium: 57% London Underground transport hubs, 36% city centre/high street, 7% retail. The mobile index captures all footfall variation, not just events.

### Data Notes

- **Stretford/Trafford Centre OA overlap** — Old Trafford football and Trafford Centre shopping share an Output Area and cannot be distinguished
- **27 Oct 2024**: partly BST→GMT clock change artefact (overnight duplication), partly half-term shopping, plus Arsenal vs Liverpool
- **Seasonal patterns**: July peak, December Christmas secondary, weekend premium largest in summer
- **POC scope**: 16,265 digital frames with playout data matched to O2 mobile index data (people counts by Output Area). Classic (paper poster) frames are excluded — playout data currently only covers digital. Industry plans exist to bring classic poster scheduling into playout systems.

### Remaining Open Questions

1. **Villa Park** — zero spike activity despite 84 nearby frames; only 1 frame carries mobile index data in the area
2. **BST→GMT clock change** — 27 Oct overnight duplication falls within playout window and may inflate indexed impacts
3. **Cardiff Six Nations** — O2 data runs Apr–Dec 2024, so Six Nations (Jan–Mar) falls outside the dataset entirely
4. **Route audience accuracy at busy venues** — if Route's models do not fully capture regular event footfall at high-frequency stadiums, both Route and the mobile index will understate the true audience

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **+4.93% net uplift** across 833 campaigns — real-world footfall variation that Route's averages smooth out
2. **73% of campaigns benefit** — broadly positive, not a zero-sum redistribution
3. **Event traffic is visible** — the hotspot map reads like a list of Premier League grounds and major venues
4. **85% of spike dates verified** — Billy Joel at Cardiff, Man Utd fixtures (75% match rate), Notting Hill Carnival (13.45x)
5. **Frame placement drives the variance** — campaigns near event venues gain 10–30%; quiet locations lose 10–20%
6. **The index measures deviation from typical patterns** — modest at busy venues where Route already captures event footfall, large at venues where events are rare (Sunderland 35x vs typical football 3–11x)
7. **Beyond events** — 58% of extreme spikes are transport hubs, high street retail, and commuter corridors
8. **Stadium data gap** — all four major stadiums assessed have OOH frames nearby but none carry mobile index data
