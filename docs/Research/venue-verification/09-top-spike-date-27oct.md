CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

The #1 spike date (27 Oct 2024, 735 spike-hours) is a COMPOSITE of:

1. DATA ARTEFACT — the dominant factor
   Liverpool and Manchester frames show identical index values for hours
   00:00-04:00 on 27 Oct, indicating the spike-hour count is substantially
   inflated by a data aggregation issue. Each overnight frame contributes
   5x the spike-hours a genuine daytime event would produce. This likely
   accounts for the majority of the apparent #1 ranking.

2. October Half-Term — Trafford Centre (Urmston Partington)
   36 Trafford Centre interior frames spike across both 26 and 27 Oct.
   October half-term (week of 28 Oct) drives unusually high Sunday shopping
   traffic at one of the UK's largest retail centres. Genuine footfall signal.

3. Arsenal 2-2 Liverpool at Emirates (Holloway, 16:30)
   The Holloway frames (peak 7.19) show a genuine match signal.
   Liverpool were AWAY from Anfield — the initial hypothesis of a home
   Liverpool match is incorrect. The signal is London-based, not Merseyside.

4. Rangers 2-1 St Mirren at Ibrox (Glasgow South, 11:00)
   The Glasgow South/Ibrox Station frames confirm a home Rangers match.

5. West Ham vs Man Utd (London Stadium, 14:00)
   Stratford/West Ham frames (2 frames, 8 spike-hours) are consistent.
   Man Utd were away — no Old Trafford home match on this date.

6. Sunderland home vs Oxford Utd (26 Oct, Sat) — peak 8.78
   Stadium of Light home attendance of 40,654 explains the Saturday spike.

SUMMARY VERDICT:
27 Oct 2024 ranks #1 primarily due to a DATA ANOMALY where overnight
hours (00-04) inherit identical high index values, inflating the spike-hour
count by approximately 5x. Genuine signals include October half-term shopping
at the Trafford Centre, the Arsenal vs Liverpool match at Emirates (Holloway),
and the Rangers home match at Ibrox. Contrary to initial hypotheses, neither
Liverpool FC nor Manchester United played at home on 27 October 2024.

RECOMMENDATION:
Investigate whether overnight hour uniformity (hours 00-04 showing identical
index values) is systematic across all dates. If so, the spike-hour ranking
methodology should filter to daytime hours (07:00-23:00) to avoid artefact
inflation. Applying this filter would likely demote 27 Oct from #1 ranking.
